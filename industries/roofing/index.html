The Reality

Running a Roofing Company Is Harder Than It Should Be

You're not short on opportunity. You're drowning in the operational weight of capturing it.

Front-End Chaos

Leads come from everywhere but nothing is connected. Your team is juggling calls, texts, and web forms while opportunities fall through the cracks.

  • Calls to the office get missed or go to voicemail
  • Web forms sit unworked for hours or days
  • Reps keep leads in their own phones and notebooks
  • Follow-up after estimates is inconsistent at best
  • Weekend and after-hours calls get lost completely

Back-End Chaos

Jobs get done, but running them is exhausting. Every month is a scramble of chasing updates, missing paperwork, and billing fire drills.

  • Jobs waiting on supplements, photos, and paperwork
  • Jobs "sold" but not scheduled
  • Jobs completed but not invoiced
  • Jobs invoiced but not followed up for payment
  • A mix of CRM, spreadsheets, texts, and emails to run it all

Coordination Overhead

Your people spend more time chasing information than doing their actual jobs. The answer always seems to be "hire more staff."

  • PMs, reps, and office staff chasing each other for updates
  • Executives pulled into "where is this job at" conversations
  • Month-end billing that eats days and multiple people
  • Owners running the business out of their inbox
  • New hires learning "how Sarah does it" instead of a system

Flying Blind

You have data everywhere but no clear picture of what's actually happening. Problems only surface after they've already hurt you.

  • Numbers only when you remember to ask
  • Each person brings their own version of the truth
  • Time spent reconciling reports instead of deciding
  • By the time you see a problem, it already hurts
  • No idea which jobs or customers are actually profitable

Real Results

What Changes in Real Life

Month-End

Month-end stops being a war room.

One contractor needed four people, including executives, to close out one department each month. Time entries were scattered, approvals were buried in email, and no one trusted the numbers. We rebuilt their internal system so time, approvals, and billing all lived in one flow. Now one person can run month-end calmly.

Coordination

Coordinators stop chasing people all day.

Another owner felt like the only solution was "hire more office staff." PMs emailed updates, reps texted the office, and finance kept a separate "jobs to invoice" list. We gave them one place where jobs move through clear stages. The same coordinator who felt underwater can now handle more volume.

Cash Flow

Jobs move, cash moves.

When jobs are tracked from sold to paid, it becomes obvious which jobs are stuck in "completed" with no invoice, or "invoiced" with no follow-up. Instead of discovering this at year-end with your CPA, you see it every week and can act on it.
